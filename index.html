ICML 2026 Social Event

How to Network at an AI Conference

Event Overview

Event: ICML 2026 Social Event: How to Network at an AI Conference

Organizers: Judah Goldfeder,Daniel Ben Levi ,Columbia University; Amit LeVi, Technion University; Philippe Wyder, University of Washington; Karime Maamari, Distyl AI; Allen Roush, Thoughtworks; and Ravid Schwartz Ziv, New York University.

Expected Participants: 150–200

A/V and Setup Requirements: Projector and screen; two handheld microphones for the organizers; round tables with 8–10 chairs each to facilitate small-group discussions.

Scope and Description

Navigating a large academic conference like ICML can be a daunting experience, particularly for those early in their careers or new to the community. The unstructured nature of coffee breaks and receptions often leads to what can only be described as “academic flocking,” where attendees gravitate toward familiar faces, inadvertently creating invisible barriers to new connections.

This social event, “How to Network at an AI Conference,” aims to demystify the art of professional connection by transforming it from an anxiety-inducing ordeal into an engaging and fruitful activity. We propose a structured yet informal session designed to equip attendees with practical, evidence-based strategies for building meaningful professional relationships.

Our goal is to move beyond the superficial exchange of pleasantries and business cards. Drawing inspiration from research on social dynamics and network theory, we will explore how genuine, memorable interactions are formed. Studies have shown that successful networking is less about the quantity of connections and more about the quality of interactions, emphasizing shared interests and authentic curiosity.

Furthermore, research suggests that adopting a growth mindset toward social skills can significantly improve outcomes in professional settings. This social will provide a low-stakes environment for like-minded participants to practice these principles, breaking down the complex dance of conference socializing into a series of manageable, and even enjoyable, steps.

Our tone will be lighthearted and slightly amusing, acknowledging the inherent awkwardness of networking while providing actionable advice to overcome it. The organizing team itself is a living embodiment of our message: five individuals from five institutions across academia and industry who connected through effective communication and networking.

Planned Activities

Total Duration: 2 hours

1. Introduction: The “Why” and “How” of Conference Networking

Duration: 15 minutes

Organizers will deliver a concise and engaging presentation, humorously addressing common networking pitfalls and offering a simple framework for conversation to foster a collaborative atmosphere.

2. Structured Icebreakers and Academic Speed-Networking

Duration: 45 minutes

Attendees will engage in brief, guided conversations in pairs or small groups using specific prompts to encourage substantive discussion.

  • Discuss a paper you have read recently that changed your perspective.
  • What is the most unexpected challenge you have faced in your research?

3. Round-Table Discussions: Thematic Deep Dives

Duration: 45 minutes

Attendees will gather at round tables, each with a designated theme. Possible discussion themes include:

  • Navigating academia versus industry
  • Finding collaborators for side projects
  • Tips for engaging with senior researchers

Table discussions will be facilitated by an organizer or volunteer, ensuring that everyone has an opportunity to contribute and fostering deeper connections based on shared professional interests and challenges.

4. Wrap-Up and Open Mingling

Duration: 15 minutes

The final portion of the event allows participants to mingle with individuals they met during the structured activities or to initiate new conversations using their newly honed skills in an unstructured setting.

This format is designed to be highly interactive, ensuring that the focus remains squarely on social connection and community building, in line with the spirit of ICML Socials.
